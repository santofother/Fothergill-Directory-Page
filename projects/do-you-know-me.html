Do You Know Me?

A game for couples and close friends. Rank a set of cards the way you'd answer, then guess how the other person ranked theirs. Score points for matching — and start some surprisingly honest conversations.

What is Do You Know Me?

Do You Know Me? is a two-player game about how well you actually understand the person across from you. Each round presents a set of cards — prompts, preferences, or scenarios — and both players privately put them in the order that reflects their own answer. Then comes the real game: you each try to guess how your partner ranked theirs. The closer your guess matches their real ranking, the more points you score. It's playful, a little revealing, and it has a way of turning "wait, that's not what I thought you'd say" into the best part of the evening.

Unlike a trivia game about facts, this is a game about a person — their tastes, their quirks, their priorities. That makes every round personal, and it means the game gets more interesting the better (or the more surprisingly) you know each other.

Why it was built

Most "couples games" fall into one of two traps: they're either dry questionnaires that feel like a quiz, or they're gimmicks that get old after one night. Do You Know Me? was built around a mechanic that stays fresh because the content is the people playing it. The ranking-and-guessing loop turns knowing someone into something you can measure, laugh about, and get better at. It's designed to spark conversation as much as competition — the moments where a guess is hilariously wrong, or unexpectedly, touchingly right, are the whole point.

How to play

  1. Draw a set of cards. Each round gives both players the same set of prompt cards to work with.
  2. Rank your own answers. Privately arrange the cards in the order that reflects how you truly feel — your favorites, your priorities, your honest take.
  3. Guess your partner's ranking. Now put the same cards in the order you think your partner ranked theirs.
  4. Reveal and score. Compare guesses against the real rankings. The closer your guess matches their actual order, the more points you earn.
  5. Play it out. Rounds add up, and the running score tells you who really knows whom — though the conversations along the way are the real reward.

Scoring and how to win

Points reward accuracy: a guess that lines up perfectly with your partner's real ranking scores highest, and near-misses still earn partial credit for getting the general order right. Because both players are guessing each other, a round can end with one person clearly "reading" the other better — or with both of you humbled. Winning is about attentiveness more than cleverness: the player who genuinely pays attention to what their partner cares about will consistently out-guess the one who's projecting their own preferences.

Tips for playing well

Guess for them, not for you

The most common mistake is ranking your partner's cards the way you would. Set your own opinion aside and ask what they would actually pick — the answers are often different in ways you'll enjoy discovering.

Remember the little tells

The offhand comment about a food they hate, the movie they always defend — those throwaway details are exactly what wins rounds. This game rewards the person who's been paying attention.

Lean into the reveals

Don't rush past a surprising result. The moments where a ranking shocks you are the best conversations the game creates — that's a feature, not a distraction.

The winner isn't the smarter player — it's the one who listens better. Do You Know Me? quietly turns attentiveness into a score.

Frequently asked questions

Is it only for couples?

It's designed with couples in mind, but it works just as well for close friends, siblings, or anyone who thinks they know each other well.

Is it free?

Yes, the game runs free in your browser with no account required.

How long does a game take?

As long as you like — rounds are quick, so you can play a couple over coffee or a long session on date night.

Do we need to be in the same place?

The game is at its best played together, side by side, so you can react to the reveals in person.

Can it work on a phone?

Yes, it's built to run in any modern mobile or desktop browser.
